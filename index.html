Introduction

“More than 98% of college-aged students use social media.” -Experian

It is very rare to come across a college student without some kind of social media platform. These networks allow users to feel in the loop with friends and family, catch up on news, and share what’s going on in their lives. However, many users of social networks experience the urge to scroll on and on, sometimes for hours. This can be particularly difficult for students during high-stress periods, like midterms or finals. This webpage will display the most popular social media networks, the correlation between number of minutes spent on social media and exam scores, and using social media in strategic ways.

Next are some ways to or use social media to your benefit or to shut off the urge to scroll in order to score in that 90-100 range!

How to Use Social Media Effectively

One of my favorite methods to stay focused during midterms/finals is to simply delete the apps for that week. But for some people, social media is a productive break from work. It’s important that we find our own social media balance. Here are some suggestions:

1. Delete the apps (just temporarily)

2. Unfollow people and clear out your feed :)

3. Take periodic social media breaks. Use a timer!

These can all help with productivity, no matter if you are a student or not. For some, “out of sight out of mind” is the best method, but for others, it’s nice to take a social media break after frying your brain for a few hours.
